piecewise_continuous

Description:

A predicate to indicate that a function is piecewise continuous everywhere, i.e. continuous at all but a finite number of points in its domain.

Example:
1/x is continuous everywhere except x=0. 
<OMOBJ xmlns="http://www.openmath.org/OpenMath">
  <OMA>
    <OMS name="piecewise_continuous" cd="fns3"/>
    <OMBIND>
      <OMS name="lambda" cd="fns1"/>
      <OMBVAR>
        <OMV name="x"/>
      </OMBVAR>
      <OMA>
        <OMS name="divide" cd="arith1"/>
        <OMI>1</OMI>
        <OMV name="x"/>
      </OMA>
    </OMBIND>
  </OMA>
</OMOBJ>

continuous_on

Description:

A predicate to indicate that a function is continuous over a particular region or range.

Example:
1/x is continuous on the real interval [1,10] 
<OMOBJ xmlns="http://www.openmath.org/OpenMath">
  <OMA>
    <OMS name="continuous_on" cd="fns3"/>
    <OMBIND>
      <OMS name="lambda" cd="fns1"/>
      <OMBVAR>
        <OMV name="x"/>
      </OMBVAR>
      <OMA>
        <OMS name="divide" cd="arith1"/>
        <OMI>1</OMI>
        <OMV name="x"/>
      </OMA>
    </OMBIND>
    <OMA>
      <OMS cd="interval1" name="interval"/>
      <OMF dec="1.0"/>
      <OMF dec="10.0"/>
    </OMA>
  </OMA>
</OMOBJ>
<math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">
 <apply><csymbol cd="fns3">continuous_on</csymbol>
  <bind><csymbol cd="fns1">lambda</csymbol>
   <bvar><ci>x</ci></bvar>

  </bind>
  <apply><csymbol cd="interval1">interval</csymbol>
