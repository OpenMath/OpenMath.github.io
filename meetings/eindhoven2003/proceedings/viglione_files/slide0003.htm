Math and E-Learning
•Computer Algebra System supports the didactical approach based on descriptive, intuitive and experimental aspects. •WWW and the e-learning explosion impose to transport  the new contents and didactical approach on the net.
Since the first time they have been introduced, Mathematica and all similar systems of
C.A. (Computer Algebra) ,have been useful to change both the way people looked
and thought about didactics in mathematics. Students are no longer passive watchers
of a process which used to be distant and unrelated to them.
The students are at the center of the learning process and they learn through a new didactical approach based on descriptive, intuitive and experimental aspects.
This means more and more interactive didactic content  and more and more user friendly software application, so the student can focus only on the contents and not on the understanding as to how to interact with the software application.
WWW and the e-learning explosion imposes to transport  the new contents and didactical approach on the net.
The step isn’t simple. To enable anyone  to access the learning contents from any place, it is necessary to follow the net standards http, html xml etc. Moreover with the actual software technology it is complicated to implement interactive and user friendly web application.
