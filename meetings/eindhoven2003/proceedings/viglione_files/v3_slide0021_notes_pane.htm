MoMAM@th has been experimented at the Faculty of Engineering of the University of Salerno. It has been used as a complementary tool for the first year mathematics students. The students attending traditional lectures have used the software for their own extra exercise sessions.
Some students have sent e-mails to teachers asking for help on how to write textually mathematic expressions with Mathematica syntax, and other students have sent comments about the fact that in MoMAM@th it’s not possible to copy and paste the graphical part of the formula.
Hence student feedback has underlined two more important limits that the MoMAM@th exercises have: they don't allow the learner to write the formulas in symbolic notation and obviously it is not allowed to copy and paste among graphical and textual parts of a formula.
