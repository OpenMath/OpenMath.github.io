How to get to Eindhoven
By plane Eindhoven has a small airport, with regular flights to Amsterdam (Schiphol) and some foreign cities. Look here for the availability of flights, or consult your local agency. From the airport you can take a taxi to the city centre (approx. Dfl 40,-).
In case you fly to Amsterdam, you have the option of taking the train (cheaper) or a connecting flight (usually faster, but often not that much) to Eindhoven.
By train There's general train information in Dutch on the web, but there's also a travel-planner. If you are looking for train connections from Amsterdam airport to Eindhoven: the airport is called Schiphol.
The train station is in the city centre. The university is on the North side of the train station; the city centre is on the South side of the train station. All hotels we have listed are on the South side of the staion, excpet for the Holiday Inn. If you have a suitcase: only the Tulip Inn, Holiday Inn and Dorint are on walking distance!
By car Drive to Eindhoven. To go to the university: Follow the signs indicating `Centrum' until you see signs indicating `Universiteit'. To go to your hotel: Follow the signs indicating `Centrum' until you see an info sign. They have a map to help you find your way. For the Tulip Inn: check out the instructions in the accommodation section. The other hotels listed have their own parking facilities.
