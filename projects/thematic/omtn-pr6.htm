Summary

During this last period of the project the Consortium completed the OpenMath 2 standard, and released a substantial collection of new Content Dictionaries. The W3C Math Working Group was reborn as a Math Interest Group with a remit to support users and represent the interests of mathematical markup to other W3C activities.

The Consortium's final workshop was held in Helsinki in May, and celebrated ten years of OpenMath.

Activity By Task

Task 1.1: Content Dictionaries

NAG and Eindhoven produced a new release, consisting of 58 brand new content dictionaries covering a range of topics in algebra, plus an update to all the existing content dictionaries to bring them into line with the OpenMath 2 standard. The new content dictionaries have been designed to support the forthcoming edition of the interactive textbook Algebra Interactive.

As requested by the reviewers, an overview document was also produced describing the new content dictionaries and the changes to the existing ones.

Task 1.2: The OpenMath Standard

Further drafts of the OpenMath 2 standard were released and discussed on the public OpenMath mailing list. A final draft of the OpenMath 2 standard was discussed at the meeting in Helsinki, and after some minor edits and corrections the finished version was released via the OpenMath Website in June. A list of the final changes made, along with a version of the document highlighting all changes since OpenMath 1.1, was also prepared.

Work has begun on an accompanying primer document, giving a gentle introduction to OpenMath for new users, but that will not be completed in the lifetime of the project.

Task 1.3: MathML/W3C Coordination

A Math Interest Group was set up by W3C in April, to maintain the existing documents, support users and liaise with other relevant groups both inside and outside W3C. One of the co-chairs is David Carlisle from NAG Ltd.

Task 2.1: Tools & Applications

An update to the Recommendations for tool developers document was produced and put on the website.

Task 3.7: Seventh Workshop

This took place in Helsinki from May 21st-22nd 2004. The slides are available on the website.

Task 3.8: Web Site

The content of the website has been updated regularly, and has been used to distribute draft versions of the OpenMath 2 standard, and to distribute slides and papers from the recent workshop. Many links have also been updated.

Self-assessment

The Consortium believes that the OpenMath 2 standard is a major step forward and will help OpenMath's adoption as part of the wider advance of XML and semantic web technologies. The new Content Dictionaries form a significant enlargement of the current collection but still more work is needed in this area, and that is where we intent to focus effort next, resources permitting.

Project Management

For reasons explained in the last managemnt report, NAG and Eindhoven took over responsibility for D16 and D20 from the University of Bath.

The Consortium continued to participate in the off-site review begun in November 2003. The final recommendations were received after the end of the project and, while no firm commitment can be made to act on them, the Consortium is grateful to the reviewers fro their constructive and encouraging tone.

Responsibility for maintaining the website and the standards documents has formally been handed over to the OpenMath Society. Mike Dewar of NAG Ltd. became Vice-President of the Society at its AGM in Helsinki in May.

In practice most partners have not been able to attend every single workshop, and European air fares have dropped substantially in the last two years, making each trip significantly less expensive than expected.
    • NAG, Stilo and INRIA had an additional travel budget to attend W3C Math Working Group meetings. We anticipated that there would be two such meetings per year, normally in North America, and budgeted 2000 Euros per trip. In fact the Math Working group has held only one meeting per year, and the first of these was in Europe.
    • NAG had an additional budget to fund new members, but few new members have been recruited to the project.
