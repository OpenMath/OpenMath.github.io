Symbols to provide a vocabulary for talking about properties of (systems of) equations.

linear

Description:

A predicate to indicate that an equation or system of equations is linear, i.e. is expressed in terms of constants and first order terms.

Example: 
<OMOBJ xmlns="http://www.openmath.org/OpenMath">
  <OMA>
    <OMS name="linear" cd="eqn1"/>
    <OMA>
      <OMS name="plus" cd="arith1"/>
      <OMV name="x"/>
      <OMI>1</OMI>
    </OMA>
  </OMA>
</OMOBJ>
<math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">
 <apply><csymbol cd="eqn1">linear</csymbol>
  <apply><csymbol cd="arith1">plus</csymbol><ci>x</ci><cn>1</cn></apply>
 </apply>
</math>
linear (plus ( x, 1) )
eqn1.linear($x + 1)

non_linear

Description:

A predicate to indicate that an equation or system of equations is non-linear, i.e. contains terms of order greater than 1.
